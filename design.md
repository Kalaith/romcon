For romantic comedy, AI is actually pretty useful at the setup layer:
generating quirky characters, flaws, goals, bad habits, secrets, jobs, social circles, and the sort of sparkling incompatibilities that make two people crash together in a fun way.

Where you will usually need to step in is the voice and charm layer:
the banter, emotional pacing, payoff, and making sure the story feels like these two specific idiots falling in love, not two cardboard cutouts following a script.

A strong way to do it would be:

1. Generate character packs, not just descriptions
Do not stop at “blonde baker, shy, loves cats.”
Have the AI create things like:

core desire

fear

biggest blind spot

everyday strength

comedic weakness

romantic weakness

job / lifestyle

secret pressure

what kind of partner they think they want

what kind they actually need

That gives you characters with engines inside them.

2. Pair for friction, not just compatibility
Good romcom couples are often not “perfect matches.”
They are usually:

emotionally complementary

practically disastrous

forced into proximity

each carrying a flaw the other accidentally pokes

A neat pairing formula is:
one person’s strength should expose the other person’s weakness.

Example:

Character A: hyper-organized, dependable, terrified of spontaneity

Character B: charming disaster, socially fearless, cannot plan breakfast
That pairing has built-in scenes before you even write chapter one.

3. Generate a premise that traps them together
The pairing matters, but the premise does the heavy lifting.
You want a situation that keeps throwing them into each other’s orbit.

Things like:

fake dating for a work or family reason

rival small businesses forced into a joint event

roommates through a housing mistake

wedding party chaos

co-owners of something inherited

one is helping the other achieve a goal while both lying about something

Romcom loves a pressure cooker.

4. Keep the novels small and clean
At 30k, you are not writing a sprawling emotional cathedral. You are building a glittering little gazebo.
That means:

one main couple

one core hook

one emotional arc per lead

one obvious obstacle

one strong finale payoff

If you try to pack in too many side plots, the story will wobble like jelly on a skateboard.

A simple 30k structure could be:

Act 1: meet cute or meet disaster, establish flaw and premise
Act 2: forced closeness, growing chemistry, comedic collisions
Midpoint: a genuine vulnerable moment, they start to matter
Act 3: misunderstanding or fear response blows things up
Finale: one grand emotional gesture, honest confession, earned ending

5. Use AI to generate options, then curate hard
This part matters a lot.
If you let AI do everything, the result may feel like it was assembled by a committee of caffeinated parrots.
Better approach:

generate 20 characters

keep the best 6

test 10 pairings

pick the 2 or 3 with the strongest tension

choose the single best premise for each pair

then outline manually

You want AI as your casting department, not your entire film studio.

Here’s a very usable generation template:

Generate 10 romantic comedy protagonists.

For each character include:
- Name
- Age
- Occupation
- Personality in 1 sentence
- 3 strengths
- 3 weaknesses
- Core desire
- Core fear
- Biggest romantic flaw
- A quirky habit
- A hidden vulnerability
- The kind of partner they think they want
- The kind of partner they actually need

Make them varied, modern, and suitable for light romantic comedy.
Avoid generic descriptions.

Then for pairing:

Take these 10 characters and suggest the best 8 romantic pairings for a romcom.

For each pairing include:
- Why they clash
- Why they secretly fit
- What trope suits them best
- A 1-sentence premise
- What emotional lesson each person needs to learn

Focus on chemistry, conflict, and comedic potential.

Then for a novella outline:

Using this pairing and premise, create a 30,000-word romantic comedy outline.

Include:
- 12 chapter beats
- key turning points
- recurring comedic motif
- emotional arc for both leads
- supporting cast roles
- final romantic payoff

Keep it warm, funny, and character-driven.

One warning bell: if every book starts from the same AI template, they may begin to feel like cousins wearing different wigs. To avoid that, vary the flavor source each time:

one book starts with profession contrast

one with family chaos

one with class difference

one with personal reinvention

one with a lie that snowballs

one with old rivals reconnecting

That keeps the little romcom machine from stamping identical heart-shaped biscuits.

This is honestly a pretty smart model for short fiction, especially if your goal is to produce a series of breezy, readable books. The best version of it would be:
AI generates the sparks, you choose the fire.
